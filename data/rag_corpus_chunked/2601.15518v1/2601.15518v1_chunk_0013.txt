Wenxin Zhou, Ritesh Mehta, and Anthony Miyaguchi 4 Discussion 4.1 Network-based Ranking One of the hypotheses in our work is that network derived fea- tures would be viable for retrieval and reranking of documents by introducing bias toward popular or well-connected items in the knowledge graph. These links are curated by editors them- selves, and so we might expect that articles are linked together semantically through categorization. Our graph construction takes these assumptions into account, where the first and second degree directed connections form the basis of a network-theoretic rank. However, the number of connected components when doing so is far too small to be effective on a small query-set of items that we are not necessarily in control of, and would require constructing our own validation or test set so that these popular items are actual part of the recall set.
