The relational structure of knowledge graphs allows for more accurate and informed retrieval. PRCA, in contrast, focuses on domain-specific abstractive summarization, using a reward-driven approach to refine the retrieved content. This strategy is designed to optimize content for the generator, particularly in scenarios where the generator functions as a black box, thereby enhancing alignment between retrieval and generation. MEMWALKER [13] presents a unique approach to handling long-context question answering by incorporating an internal search and ranking mechanism within a memory tree structure. This method navigates extensive memory stores, ensuring that the most relevant information is retrieved and used for complex queries.
