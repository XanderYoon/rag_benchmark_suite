Information Retrieval Journal 1 3 8.1 Structured document retrieval The information retrieval field has presented multiple methods to incorporate the internal organization of a given document into indexing and retrieval steps. The pro- gress in document design and storage has resulted in new representations for docu- ments, known as structured documents (Chiaramella, 2000), such as HTML and XML, where the document has multiple fields. Considering the structure of a document when designing retrieval models can usually improve retrieval results (Wilkinson, 1994). Zamani et al, (2018) proposed a neural ranking model that extracts the document rep- resentation from the aggregation of field-level representations and then uses a match- ing network to predict the final relevance score. Beyond Web pages, a table itself can also be considered as a structured document.
