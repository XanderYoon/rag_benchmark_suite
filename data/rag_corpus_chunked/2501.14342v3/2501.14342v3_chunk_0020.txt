The intermediate retrieval chains are automatically generated via rejection sampling, thereby alleviating the need for manual annotation. At test time, we offer multiple decoding strategies to manage the trade-off between performance and compute. Our experiments demonstrate that CoRAG-8B achieves state-of-the-art performance on both multi- hop QA datasets and the KILT benchmark, surpassing many baselines built with larger LLMs. A comprehensive analysis is conducted to understand its scaling behavior and generalization capability.
